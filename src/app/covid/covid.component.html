COVID 19 Resource

Telemedicine is committed to the health and safety of our employees and our communities. The work we do and the world at large is impacted by COVID-19 and we're taking steps to mitigate the crisis. Your health and safety is our priority. We follow all mandated city, state and federal CDC guidelines.


Please review the important information below ahead of your visit (In-Person)

If you are feeling ill, please stay home and contact your health professional. Signs of COVID-19 illness include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Cough
  • Fever of 100.4 or higher
  • Runny nose or new sinus congestion
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Chills
  • Sore Throat
  • New loss of taste and/or smell
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Diarrhea

Steps Taken

In order to protect our patients, doctors, and staff we have taken the steps needed in doing so:

  • All doctors and staff must be willing to be vaccinated.
  • In alignment with local and state health regulations, masks are no longer required by patients, but they are still strongly recommended. Doctors and staff will continue to do.
  • We continue to stay updated on world news and adjust.
  • For your convenience, hand sanitizer stations have been placed throughout our building
  • Patients and staff are encouraged to frequently wash their hands with soap and hot water or hand sanitizer for at least 20 seconds.
  • Cleaning procedures have been enhanced for all high-touch areas.
  • We offer for online meetings with our patients if they don't feel comfortable in coming in-person
